# Structures to Avoid

## Binary Contrasts

State the point directly. Drop the negation.

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| "Not because X. Because Y." | Telegraphed reversal |
| "[X] isn't the problem. [Y] is." | Formulaic reframe |
| "The answer isn't X. It's Y." | Predictable pivot |
| "not X, it's Y" / "isn't X, it's Y" | Mechanical contrast |

**Fix:** State Y directly. "Y matters here."

## Negative Listing

Listing what something is NOT before revealing what it IS.

**Fix:** State the thing. The reader doesn't need the runway.

## Dramatic Fragmentation

Sentence fragments for manufactured emphasis.

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| "[Noun]. That's it. That's the [thing]." | Performative simplicity |
| "X. And Y. And Z." | Staccato drama |

**Fix:** Complete sentences. Trust content over presentation.

## False Agency

AI loves giving inanimate things human verbs to avoid naming the actor.

| Pattern | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "a complaint becomes a fix" | Someone fixed it |
| "the decision emerges" | Someone decides |
| "the culture shifts" | People change behavior |
| "the data tells us" | Someone reads the data |
| "the market rewards" | Buyers pay for things |

**Fix:** Name the human. "The team fixed it" beats "the complaint becomes a fix."

## Passive Voice

| Pattern | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "X was created" | Name who created it |
| "Mistakes were made" | Name who made them |
| "The decision was reached" | Name who decided |

## Rhythm Traps

| Pattern | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Three-item lists | Two items or one |
| Questions answered immediately | Let questions breathe or cut them |
| Every paragraph ends punchily | Vary endings |
| Em-dashes | Remove. Use commas or periods. |
