# Alkira Objection Handling Reference

Use these responses when prepping for calls, drafting emails in response to pushback, or coaching on common objections.

---

## 1. "We're happy with what we have"

**Approach A — Integration angle:**
Acknowledge their current tools. Position Alkira as enhancing what they already have, not replacing everything. Alkira integrates with existing SD-WAN, firewalls, and security investments — it connects and consolidates them rather than ripping and replacing.

**Approach B — Visibility angle:**
Even with good tools, most enterprises lack unified visibility and connectivity across all environments. Alkira provides the connective tissue and single pane of glass that point solutions can't.

---

## 2. "We're already in the cloud"

Being in the cloud is the starting point, not the finish line. Most enterprises in the cloud still lack consistent network and security posture across all their cloud services, regions, and on-prem environments. Ask which clouds and regions they use — Alkira provides consistent networking and security across all of them.

---

## 3. "Don't have time / budget"

Acknowledge competing priorities. Then use social proof: Koch Industries (Fortune 100) found that Alkira actually *saved* time and resources by eliminating the operational burden of managing disparate networking tools. Redirect to understanding their biggest time/budget drains — Alkira often addresses the root cause.

---

## 4. "What is Alkira?"

Simple explanation: Alkira does for networking and security what AWS and Azure did for compute and storage. It delivers network infrastructure on-demand as-a-service — consistent security posture across premises, remote users, data centers, and clouds, with unified visibility from a single pane of glass.

---

## 5. "Just send me an email"

**Approach A — Qualify first:** Before sending, ask what initiatives or challenges would be most relevant so you can tailor the information. This turns a brush-off into a discovery moment.

**Approach B — Email + follow-up:** Agree to send information, but suggest scheduling a brief 15-minute follow-up call with an engineer who can speak to their specific environment. This keeps the conversation alive.

---

## 6. "I'm not the decision maker"

**Approach A — Learn from them:** Even if they don't sign the check, they understand the day-to-day challenges better than anyone. Learn about their environment and pain points so you can frame the conversation properly when you reach leadership.

**Approach B — Validate fit first:** Appeal to their expertise — they're the ones who know whether something like Alkira would actually work in their environment. Get their technical buy-in before approaching leadership.

---

## 7. "Not interested"

Acknowledge the skepticism — cold calls are annoying. Ask for just 15 minutes with an engineer who can show them something specific to their environment. If after 15 minutes they're still not interested, you'll walk away. Low commitment ask.

---

## 8. "Are you secure?"

Yes. Alkira provides end-to-end encryption, comprehensive compliance capabilities, and deep visibility and control over all network traffic. Security is integrated into the platform (not bolted on), with third-party security service integration from the marketplace. Customers maintain full ownership and governance of their network while Alkira manages the underlying infrastructure.

---

## 9. "We don't have time to implement"

Alkira is specifically designed for rapid deployment — customers go from design to production in minutes, not months. Full team support during onboarding. Smooth transitions with minimal disruption to existing operations. No hardware to install, no software to configure per-site.

---

## 10. "How is Alkira different?"

Four things that set Alkira apart:
1. **Cloud-native architecture** — not an overlay or orchestration layer
2. **Seamless multi-cloud connectivity** without complex hardware or deep cloud expertise
3. **Simplified management** — single pane of glass for the entire network
4. **Enhanced visibility** — see and control everything across on-prem, cloud, and remote environments

The short version: Alkira is the only vendor providing true end-to-end network infrastructure as-a-service. No one else solves both cloud and traditional network use cases in a single platform.

---

## 11. "I'm busy, call me back later"

Ask for 30 seconds to explain why this is relevant to them specifically. If it resonates, schedule a proper time. If not, you've saved both of you a future call.
